Fresh Fruit Detection

India's diverse climate ensures availability of all varieties of fresh fruits and vegetables. It ranks second in fruit production in the world. As per National Horticulture Database published by National Horticulture Board, during 2018-19, India produced 90.2 million metric tons of fruits. Amongst fruits, India ranks first in production of Bananas (25.7%), Papayas (43.6%) and Mangoes (40.4%). Quality of fruits determines the shelf life as fresh commodities have a very high importance in their post-harvest handling and supply chain management. Grievously, bananas and mangoes are artificially ripened as they are consumed in large quantities. These produced fruits contain pesticides that may harm human beings to a greater extent. This can be overcome only by the selection of the quality of fruit and the only way to do it is Quality Detection using the latest technologies.This can probably ensure organic consumption of fruits and vegetables, and greatly helps horticulture and agro-industries to enhance trading of fruits with good quality. The product can play a significant role in terms of Make in India Scheme, where the model is designed to classify the fruits effectively.
